GROUP EXTENSIONS

ISHAN LEVY
How can we classify extensions of a group \(G\) by \(H\)?

This problem can be understood from the point of view of homotopy theory by interpreting our groups as automorphisms of some type of object, so that \(BG\) becomes the groupoid of objects of that type. Then giving an extension \(1 \to H \to E \to G \to 1\) is the same as giving a pointed ﬁbre sequence \(BE \to BG\), with ﬁbre \(BH\). Such ﬁbrations correspond to pointed maps \(BG \to B\Aut (BH)\), giving the classiﬁcation. A split extension is the same as a pointed ﬁbre sequence \(BE \to BG\) with a section, which corresponds to a pointed map from \(BG \to B\Aut _*(BH)\) where \(\Aut _*\) denotes automorphisms as a pointed space.

For example, extensions of \(\ZZ \) by \(H\) are the same as pointed maps \(B\ZZ \to B\Aut (BH)\) which is just \(\Aut (BH)\). \(\pi _0(\Aut (BH))\) is the same as the outer automorphisms \(\Out (H)\), giving the equivalence classes of such extensions.

The ﬁbre of the map \(\Aut (BH) \to \Out (H)\) is the space of automorphisms of \(BH\) identiﬁable with the identity, which is the same as \(B\Aut _{\Aut (BH)} 1\). Delooping the group homorphism \(\Aut (BH) \to \Out (H)\), we see there is a ﬁbre sequence \(B^2\Aut _{\Aut (BH)} 1 \to B\Aut (BH) \to B\Out (H)\).

Conjugation by an element of \(H\) is a natural transformation of the identity on \(BH\), giving a homomorphism \(f:H \to \Aut (BH)\). The center \(Z(H)\) is the ﬁxed points of this action, i.e a path out of a ﬁxed point \(x\), \(f:x \to a \in BH\) and an identiﬁcation of the action of \(H\) on \(f\) with the trivial action. For a given path, we can identify such identiﬁcations with automorphisms of the identity on \(BH\), so \(BZ(H) = B\Aut _{\Aut (BH)} 1\).

Thus, there is a ﬁbre sequence \(B^2Z(H) \to B\Aut (BH) \to B\Out (H)\). Now suppose \(G,H\) are \(1\)-groups (A weaker condition on \(G\) might also do). Given a central extension, or a pointed map \(BG \to B\Aut (BH)\), the action of \(G\) on \(H\) is the induced action on the loop spaces of the base points. That action is trivial iﬀ the delooped map is trivial when \(1\)-truncated. But the \(1\)-truncation is \(B\Out (H)\), so from the ﬁbre sequence we see that central extensions correspond to pointed maps \(BG \to B^2 Z(H) = K(H,2)\). In particular, equivalence classes of central extension correspond to \(H^2(BG;H)\).
